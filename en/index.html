How are passwords generated ?

+

Passwords are generated securely and privately on your own computer. When you request the generation of a password, the process takes place only in your web browser. No password is transmitted over the Internet or stored on our servers.

To accomplish this, we use a cryptographically secure random number generation method provided by the browser, called window.crypto.getRandomValues(). This method generates random numbers from a random data source of your computer’s operating system. These random numbers are then used to choose the characters that make up your password.

In summary, you can be assured that the passwords generated on this site are unique, random and available only on your browser. No information about these passwords is sent to our servers or to other locations, which ensures the privacy and security of your information.

What are the benefits of using a random password ?

+

A random password is harder to guess or hack than a password based on personal words or dates. It therefore offers better protection of your accounts and online data.

What password length is recommended ?

+

Passwords should be at least 12 characters long to ensure maximum security. The longer the password, the harder it is to guess or break.

How do I remember or store my passwords ?

+

It's not easy to remember several random passwords. You can use a password manager, which is an application or service that automatically stores and fills in your passwords on the websites you visit. You then only need to remember one master password to access your manager. There are several free and paid password managers, such as Proton Pass, Dashlane and Bitwarden.

What are the mistakes not to make with passwords ?

+

There are several mistakes to avoid with passwords, such as:

  • Use the same password for multiple accounts
  • Use words or dates that are easy to guess (name, birthday, animal …)
  • Share your passwords with other people
  • Write your passwords on a paper or in an unsecured file
  • Click on suspicious links or attachments that could steal your passwords

Do I have to change my passwords regularly?

+

Changing your passwords regularly is good security practice, but not always necessary if you already use strong, unique passwords for each account. Be sure to change your passwords if you suspect a compromise.

How can I protect my accounts in addition to my passwords ?

+

In addition to using strong passwords, there are several complementary practices you can put in place to strengthen the security of your online accounts. Here are a few to consider :

  1. Two-factor authentication (2FA):
    2FA adds an extra layer of security by requiring a second method of identification after the password. This can include a code sent to your phone, an authentication app or a physical security key. Even if someone gets your password, they won’t be able to access your account without the second method of authentication.
  2. Security keys:
    Security keys are physical devices that generate unique authentication codes. They offer increased security over 2FA methods based on SMS or apps, because they are less likely to be hacked.
  3. Recovery codes:
    When setting up 2FA, many services offer you to generate recovery codes. These codes can be used to access your account if you lose access to your second method of authentication. Make sure to store these codes in a safe place.
  4. Two-step verification:
    In addition to 2FA, some platforms offer two-step verification. This involves sending a verification code to your email address or phone when you log in from a new device or browser.
  5. Account activity monitoring:
    Enable notifications to be alerted of unusual activities on your account, such as logins from unusual locations. This allows you to react quickly if your account is compromised.
  6. Regular updates:
    Make sure your software, apps and operating systems are up to date with the latest security updates to minimize potential vulnerabilities.
  7. Security awareness:
    Educate yourself on common online threats such as phishing and brute force attacks. Be skeptical of suspicious emails or links and never share your credentials.

By combining these practices with strong passwords, you can significantly reduce the risk of your online accounts being compromised and protect your personal information.
